How to prepare your T1-MRI

1. Check the MRI orientation
  • Make sure that all the MRI views (axial, coronal, sagittal) correspond with their titles, and that the L/R (left/right) and A/P (anterior/posterior) markers are correctly placed.
  • If you have to fix the MRI volume, right-click on the MRI Viewer > Figure > Edit volume. Then:
  • Click as many times as needed on any "ZXY" button (permutation of dimensions).
  • Use the rotate and flip buttons to align all the views according to the L/R and A/P indicators.
2. Check left / right
  • Make sure that the left part of the axial image is really the left hemisphere.
3. Select the SCS and MNI fiducials
  • To select a fiducial: move the cross to the point you want to select, and then click on the "Set" button of the fiducial you want to define
  • Contrast adjustment: right-click and move mouse up/down.
  • NAS: Nasion
  • LPA: Left pre-auricular point
  • RPA: Right pre-auricular
  • AC: Anterior commissure
  • PC: Posterior commissure
  • IH: Any inter-hemispheric point
